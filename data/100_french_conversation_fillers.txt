100 FRENCH CONVERSATION FILLERS
=================================
Master these to sound natural and buy yourself time while speaking

These fillers (tics de langage) are essential for natural-sounding French. They help you pause, 
emphasize, transition, and generally sound like a native speaker rather than a textbook learner.

===================================

CATEGORY 1: HESITATION FILLERS (When you need a moment to think)
-----------------------------------------------------------------
1. euh - uh, um (THE most common - you'll hear this constantly)
2. euh ben - uh well
3. heu - um (alternate spelling of euh)
4. mmm - mmm
5. hum - hmm
6. eh - eh
7. ah - ah
8. oh - oh
9. ouh - ooh
10. bah - well (indicates hesitation or indifference)
11. ben - well (informal version of "bien")
12. pff - pff (exasperation or indifference)

===================================

-----------------------------------------

14. bon - well, good (signals transition or ending)
15. donc - so, therefore
16. du coup - so, as a result (very trendy, overused by French people)
17. eh bien - well (more formal than "ben")
18. bon ben - okay then, alright
19. bon alors - so then, well then
20. et puis - and then
21. et donc - and so
22. et alors - and so, so what
23. écoute - listen (to get attention)
24. écoutes - listen (variant)
25. dis donc - I say, wow
26. tiens - hey, look (to grab attention)
27. tenez - here, take this (formal version)
28. dites donc - I say (formal)
29. attends - wait
30. attendez - wait (formal)

===================================

HOW TO USE THESE EFFECTIVELY
-----------------------------

1. DON'T FORCE IT: These need to feel natural. Start with 3-5 that feel comfortable.

2. LISTEN FIRST: Pay attention to how French people use them in context (podcasts, TV, 
   conversations). Notice the intonation and placement.

3. START SIMPLE: Begin with the most common ones:
   - euh (hesitation)
   - alors (starting/transitioning)
   - bon (ending/transitioning)
   - voilà (concluding/agreeing)
   - quoi (at end for emphasis)

4. AGE-APPROPRIATE: Some are generational:
   - Genre, grave, en vrai = younger people (under 35)
   - Bon, alors, donc = all ages
   - Du coup = overused by millennials (French people joke about this)

5. FORMALITY MATTERS:
   - INFORMAL: euh, ben, ouais, quoi, genre, grave
   - NEUTRAL: alors, donc, en fait, voilà
   - MORE FORMAL: eh bien, effectivement, évidemment

6. REGIONAL VARIATIONS: Be aware that usage varies by region. Listen to people around 
   you in France and mirror what you hear.

7. DON'T OVERUSE: Even native speakers get criticized for using too many fillers 
   (especially "du coup" and "genre"). Use them naturally, not every sentence.

===================================

PRONUNCIATION TIPS
------------------

• euh = like "euh" with rounded lips, like German ö [ø]
• hein = sounds like "un" (one) with nasal vowel
• bon = nasal "on" sound
• ben = nasal "en" sound  
• quoi = "kwa"
• genre = "zhonr" (soft j, nasal vowel)
• bof = "boff"

===================================

CULTURAL NOTE
-------------
French people are VERY aware of their filler word habits and often make fun of them. 
"Du coup" in particular has become a joke because millennials use it constantly. If you 
can playfully acknowledge you're using fillers, it shows you're aware of the culture:

"Je dis trop 'du coup', désolé!" - I say 'du coup' too much, sorry!

The best approach: Listen more than you speak at first. You'll naturally pick up the 
rhythm and start using these without thinking about it.
